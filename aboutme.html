The city of Chester.

Nestled between the mountains of Snowdonia and the plains of Cheshire, located where the river Dee once met the Irish Sea, practically on the tripoint of Wales, the West Midlands, and the North of England, stands the magnificent little city of Chester.

Founded by the Romans, extended by the Saxons, captured by the Normans, besieged by the Parliamentarians, gentrified by the Georgians, and connected to the canal and railway networks by the Victorians, it is the place I am very privileged to be able to call home.

Theatre in the Quarter (TiQ).

TiQ is a drama company based in my hometown of Chester, heavily focussed on providing stage experience to children and the local community.

They have three children’s troupes: Jigsaw Music Theatre (for ages 7–13), Quartz Youth Theatre (for ages 13–19), and Rewind Youth Theatre (for ages 6–11).

I myself was a member of Jigsaw for many years, performing in sixteen plays. Quartz was formed when I was in my final year of high school, so I was in Quartz for their début production, “Masque”, also.

But I’m not a great actor. I prefer my current situation, where I’m currently able to contribute in several different ways.

On leaving Jigsaw in 2012, I became Jigsaw’s assistant-director and designed the poster and programme for their play “Ghosts of the City”. I have now designed around two dozen posters (and around two dozen programmes) for Jigsaw, Quartz, and Rewind.

I didn’t mention I write lyrics. TiQ always has songs in its productions, and I’ve lyricised many of them. That’s possibly what I’m best at.

My next project with TiQ is Rewind’s play “The Mystery Express”, about a train returning to Chester after decades of absence. What happened to it? Why has it come back?
